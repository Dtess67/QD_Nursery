- One Flat-Earth evaluation produced `SUPPORTED 0.82`; its repetition
  produced `UNCERTAIN 0.22`.
- The turtle-and-elephants satire claim produced `CONTESTED 0.20` in one
  run and `SUPPORTED 0.82` in another.
- The alcohol-health claim changed from `REFUTED 0.88` to
  `SUPPORTED 0.88`.
- The plain MMR claim produced `SUPPORTED 0.90` twice, while the same
  proposition prefaced with "Despite anti-vaccine claims" produced
  `CONTESTED 0.28` and `CONTESTED 0.27`.
- Retrieval-set variance occurred in multiple repeated live cases.
  Therefore live-run changes cannot automatically be attributed to model
  reasoning alone.
- The Falsifier rejected every live evaluation.

### Controlled lane

  Evidence order therefore affected the current funnel's behavior.
- Some repeated controlled evaluations changed despite using one fixed

- Falsifier approval also changed between repeated controlled evaluations.
- One source-family copy versus three identical copies produced essentially

  not demonstrate duplicate-source confidence inflation.

## Architectural interpretation

The benchmark establishes that the funnel mechanics execute, but does not
earn survivor polarity the authority to author a truth verdict.

The current model-generated hypothesis base is non-exhaustive, order
sensitive, and capable of producing confident support for obvious
falsehoods. Confidence does not presently demonstrate stability or
calibration.

The funnel may remain as an auditable diagnostic and possibility-generation
instrument. Eliminated hypotheses and their eliminating observations must
remain preserved in the ledger. Survivor count must not become truth or
confidence merely because alternatives were removed.

Disagreement diagnosis must not assign a certified causal label such as
`POSITIONAL` or `REASONING`. It should maintain versioned possible
explanations with only:

- `ELIMINATED`
- `SURVIVING`
- `UNTESTED`

The final surviving explanation remains a suspect, not a conviction.
